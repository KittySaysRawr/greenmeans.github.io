What Does Green Mean?

Green New Deal: "A transformative economic programme to tackle the climate emergency and create over a million good green jobs in the process."
  • £40 billion annual investment to create 1.2 million jobs in renewable energy, public transport, home insulation and nature restoration
    • Creates 5-7 jobs per £1 million invested compared to 2.7 jobs in fossil fuels (University of Massachusetts)
    • Addresses both climate crisis and economic inequality simultaneously
    • Builds resilient infrastructure that saves money long-term through reduced energy costs
    • Positions UK as global leader in green technology and sustainable development
    Source: Green Party UK, UN Sustainable Development Goals, University of Massachusetts "Green Growth" study
  • Green apprenticeships creating pathways into renewable energy, retrofitting and environmental conservation careers
    • Addresses skills gap in growing green sectors where demand exceeds supply
    • Provides stable, well-paid careers with progression opportunities
    • Ensures UK workforce is prepared for the global transition to green economy
    • Reduces youth unemployment while building essential national infrastructure
    Source: Green Party Skills Policy, OECD Green Jobs Report, UK Renewable Energy Association
  • Just Transition for workers in high-carbon industries with guaranteed retraining and new green job opportunities
    • Prevents communities being left behind in the transition to net-zero
    • Builds broad public support for climate action by ensuring no one is disadvantaged
    • Transfers valuable skills from traditional industries to new green sectors
    • International best practice from Germany's Ruhr Valley transition shows this approach works
    Source: Green Party Just Transition Policy, International Labour Organization, German Energy Transition Case Studies
Public Investment for Public Good: "Redirecting finance to serve people and planet, not private profit. Our investment policies ensure essential services are publicly owned and democratically controlled."
  • Renationalise energy companies to create a publicly owned, democratically accountable energy system
    • Eliminates £2.7 billion in annual private profits that could be reinvested in renewable energy
    • Enables coordinated national planning for rapid transition to 100% renewable energy by 2030
    • Reduces consumer bills by 10-15% by removing profit motive from essential service
    • Creates 400,000 stable public sector jobs in renewable energy and grid modernization
    Source: Green Party Energy Policy, Public Ownership Project, Common Wealth Think Tank
  • Public ownership of water companies to end sewage dumping and invest in infrastructure
    • Ends £2.4 billion annual dividend payments to shareholders since privatisation
    • Enables £10 billion investment in water infrastructure without profit extraction
    • Stops raw sewage discharges that have increased by 2,553% since privatisation
    • Reduces water bills by 20% while improving service quality and environmental protection
    Source: Green Party Water Policy, We Own It, University of Greenwich Public Services International Research Unit
  • Public control of public transport including railways and bus networks
    • Saves £1.5 billion annually in private profit extraction from public transport
    • Enables integrated ticketing and coordinated service planning across regions
    • Reduces fares by 10% making public transport accessible to all income levels
    • Accelerates transition to electric fleets through coordinated public investment
    Source: Green Party Transport Policy, Transport for Quality of Life, Campaign for Better Transport
Real Democracy for All: "A democratic system that works for everyone, not just the wealthy and powerful. We will transform our political system to give power back to people and communities."
  • Abolish the monarchy and establish a democratic republic with an elected head of state
    • Ends hereditary privilege and establishes equality before the law for all citizens
    • Saves £345 million annually in sovereign grant and security costs
    • Creates democratic accountability for head of state rather than birthright privilege
    • Aligns UK with modern democratic nations like Germany, Ireland and France
    Source: Green Party Republican Policy, Republic Campaign, TaxPayers' Alliance
  • Replace House of Lords with elected Senate representing nations and regions
    • Ends undemocratic appointments and hereditary peerages
    • Creates regional representation to balance London-centric politics
    • Reduces costs by 30% through smaller, more efficient elected chamber
    • Improves legislative scrutiny through democratic mandate and expertise
    Source: Green Party Constitutional Reform Policy, Electoral Reform Society, University College London Constitution Unit
  • Proportional representation for all elections to ensure every vote counts
    • Ends majority governments elected by minority of voters
    • Ensures parliamentary representation matches public voting patterns
    • Encourages cooperation and consensus across political parties
    • Increases voter turnout by 5-10% as all votes have equal value
    Source: Green Party Electoral Reform Policy, Make Votes Matter, International IDEA
  • Reform NATO into a collective security organisation focused on conflict prevention
    • Redirects military spending to climate security and human needs
    • Creates inclusive security framework including Russia and neutral states
    • Focuses on root causes of conflict like resource scarcity and inequality
    • Aligns with UN Charter principles of collective security and peaceful resolution
    Source: Green Party Peace & Security Policy, Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament, UN Institute for Disarmament Research
  • Community democracy with local people controlling planning and budgets
    • Increases civic participation with 20-30% higher engagement in decision-making
    • Ensures development meets local needs rather than corporate interests
    • Builds community cohesion and local problem-solving capacity
    • Successful models like participatory budgeting show higher satisfaction rates
    Source: Green Party Local Democracy Policy, New Economics Foundation, Participatory Budgeting Project
  • Digital democracy with online platforms for citizen participation
    • Enables continuous citizen input between elections
    • Increases accessibility for disabled people and those with caring responsibilities
    • Reduces costs of traditional consultation methods by 40%
    • Successful models in Estonia and Taiwan show high participation rates
    Source: Green Party Digital Democracy Policy, mySociety, OECD Digital Government Studies
Climate Emergency & Nature Restoration: "The climate crisis is the greatest threat humanity has ever faced. We will take the action needed to limit heating to 1.5°C while restoring our natural world for future generations."
  • Net zero by 2040 at the latest, with ambitious 75% reduction by 2030 from 1990 levels
    • Avoids £20 billion annually in climate damage costs by 2050 (Climate Change Committee)
    • Creates 1.2 million jobs in renewable energy and energy efficiency
    • Improves air quality saving NHS £1.7 billion annually in respiratory treatment
    • Positions UK as global climate leader attracting green investment
    Source: Green Party Climate Policy, Climate Change Committee, Lancet Countdown
  • Ban all new fossil fuel projects including oil and gas extraction, fracking and coal mining
    • Existing fossil fuel reserves already exceed carbon budget for 1.5°C (IPCC)
    • Prevents £11 billion in stranded assets as world transitions from fossil fuels
    • Reduces air pollution preventing 40,000 premature deaths annually in UK
    • Accelerates renewable investment creating more sustainable jobs
    Source: Green Party Fossil Fuel Policy, IPCC Reports, Carbon Tracker Initiative
  • 100% renewable electricity by 2030 through massive expansion of wind, solar and tidal power
    • Ends dependence on volatile international gas markets saving £50 billion on imports
    • Creates 400,000 jobs in renewable energy manufacturing and installation
    • Reduces electricity costs long-term as renewables become cheapest energy source
    • Builds UK expertise in growing global renewable energy market
    Source: Green Party Energy Policy, International Renewable Energy Agency, Energy & Climate Intelligence Unit
  • 30% of land and sea protected for nature by 2030 with strong legal protections
    • UK has lost 56% of biodiversity since 1970 - urgent action needed (State of Nature Report)
    • Natural habitats store carbon equivalent to 10 years of UK emissions
    • Creates 20,000 jobs in conservation and land management
    • Protects ecosystem services worth £30 billion annually to UK economy
    Source: Green Party Nature Policy, State of Nature Report, Natural Capital Committee
  • End sewage dumping in rivers with mandatory monitoring and fines
    • Water companies discharged raw sewage 400,000 times in 2020 alone
    • Clean rivers support £2.6 billion angling industry and water sports
    • Protects public health from waterborne diseases and antibiotic resistance
    • Creates 5,000 jobs in water monitoring and river restoration
    Source: Green Party Water Policy, Environment Agency data, Surfers Against Sewage
  • Rewilding and reforestation planting 700 million trees by 2030
    • Creates 15,000 jobs in forestry and land management
    • Captures 15 million tonnes of CO2 annually helping meet climate targets
    • Restores wildlife corridors and reverses species decline
    • Reduces flood risk through natural water management saving £3 billion in damages
    Source: Green Party Tree Policy, Woodland Trust, Committee on Climate Change
Health for All: "Restoring our NHS as a comprehensive, publicly owned service free at the point of use."
  • £8 billion annual funding increase for the NHS to restore services, reduce waiting times and pay staff fairly
    • Brings UK health spending in line with European average of 10.5% of GDP
    • Reduces costly delayed discharges and emergency admissions through better preventative care
    • Investing in health creates economic returns through healthier, more productive workforce
    • Every £1 spent on NHS generates £4 in economic activity through job creation and supplier spending
    Source: Green Party NHS Policy, The King's Fund, Office for National Statistics
  • End NHS privatisation and bring all outsourced services back in-house
    • Private providers cost 10-15% more than equivalent NHS services (NHS Confederation)
    • Eliminates profit extraction from healthcare, redirecting funds to patient care
    • Improves accountability and democratic control over health services
    • Creates integrated care pathways rather than fragmented profit-driven services
    Source: Green Party NHS Policy, NHS Confederation, British Medical Association
  • Staff pay restoration to properly value healthcare workers after years of real-terms pay cuts
    • Reduces staff turnover saving £100-200 million annually on recruitment and training
    • Improves patient safety through experienced, motivated workforce with lower vacancy rates
    • Addresses current crisis where 1 in 8 NHS posts are vacant affecting patient care
    • Fair pay reduces burnout and improves staff wellbeing leading to better care
    Source: Green Party NHS Staff Policy, NHS Employers, Health Foundation
Strong Local Communities: "Building communities where everyone has a home, support when they need it, and power over their local area."
  • 150,000 new social homes built every year to the highest environmental standards
    • Saves £1.5 billion annually in housing benefit costs by providing affordable social housing
    • Creates 150,000 construction jobs and supports local supply chains
    • Reduces homelessness saving £26,000 per person annually in emergency services
    • Improves health outcomes through stable, warm housing reducing NHS costs
    Source: Green Party Housing Policy, Shelter, National Housing Federation
  • Rent controls linking increases to average wages or inflation, whichever is lower
    • Prevents displacement and maintains community stability
    • Puts £2.8 billion back into local economies through reduced housing costs
    • Scottish experience shows rent controls can work without reducing supply
    • Reduces pressure on local services through stable communities
    Source: Green Party Rent Control Policy, Scottish Government Evaluation, Joseph Rowntree Foundation
  • Community power giving local people control over planning and local budgets
    • Increases civic engagement and community cohesion
    • Ensures development meets local needs rather than developer profits
    • Successful models like participatory budgeting show 20% higher satisfaction
    • Builds local resilience and capacity for self-governance
    Source: Green Party Community Power Policy, Participatory Budgeting Project, New Local Government Network

  • £10 billion investment in cycling and walking infrastructure in every town and city

    • Reduces NHS costs by £17 billion through increased physical activity
    • Creates 25,000 jobs in construction and maintenance
    • Boosts local high streets as cycling customers visit more frequently

    • Aligns with UK's international human rights obligations and moral responsibilities

    • Prevents tragic deaths in Channel crossings - 300+ since 1999

    • Enables proper screening and integration support for new arrivals

Evidence-Based Policy: "Our policies are grounded in rigorous research, scientific evidence, and successful international examples. We address common misconceptions with facts, data, and real-world examples of Green policies working effectively."
  • "Green policies are too expensive" - Actually they save money long-term and create economic opportunities
    • Climate inaction costs UK economy £20-30 billion annually already (Climate Change Committee)
    • Every £1 invested in green infrastructure generates £3-7 in economic benefits (World Bank)
    • Renewable energy now cheaper than fossil fuels - solar costs fallen 89% in decade (International Renewable Energy Agency)
    • Green New Deal pays for itself through job creation, reduced healthcare costs, and energy savings
    • UK spends £10.5 billion annually subsidising fossil fuels vs £7 billion on renewables (IMF)
    Sources: Climate Change Committee, World Bank, International Renewable Energy Agency, International Monetary Fund, Office for Budget Responsibility
  • "We can't afford the Green New Deal" - Actually we can't afford not to implement it
    • COVID pandemic showed UK can mobilise £400 billion when facing existential threats
    • Climate crisis represents greater long-term economic threat than pandemic
    • Green investment generates returns through job creation (1.2 million jobs), energy savings, and reduced environmental damage
    • Current economic model costs more through pollution, health impacts, and climate damage
    • Progressive taxation on wealthiest (top 1% own 23% of UK wealth) can fund transition fairly
    Sources: Office for National Statistics, Institute for Fiscal Studies, New Economics Foundation, OXFAM UK Wealth Distribution Report
  • "Green policies will destroy jobs" - Evidence shows they create more and better jobs
    • Renewable energy creates 3x more jobs per £1 million invested than fossil fuels (University of Massachusetts)
    • UK's offshore wind sector already supports 26,000 jobs, projected to reach 69,800 by 2026 (ORE Catapult)
    • Energy efficiency retrofitting could create 500,000 skilled jobs across UK regions
    • Just Transition policies ensure no worker left behind with retraining and new opportunities
    • Green jobs typically higher skilled, better paid, and more unionised than fossil fuel equivalents
    Sources: University of Massachusetts "Green Growth" study, Offshore Renewable Energy Catapult, TUC, Energy Saving Trust
  • "Renewables can't power modern society" - Evidence shows they can and already are
    • UK has best wind resources in Europe - potential to power country several times over (Carbon Trust)
    • Scotland already generates 97% of electricity from renewables, showing technical feasibility
    • Smart grids, battery storage, and interconnectors solve intermittency issues
    • Renewables enhance energy security by reducing dependence on imported fossil fuels
    • Multiple studies confirm 100% renewable energy systems technically and economically feasible by 2050 (Stanford University, LUT University)
    Sources: Carbon Trust, Scottish Government, National Grid ESO, Stanford University "100% Clean and Renewable" study
  • "Nuclear power is necessary for baseload" - Evidence shows it's unnecessary and uneconomic
    • Hinkley Point C will cost £22-£23 billion - most expensive power station in world history
    • Strike price of £92.50/MWh (2012 prices) vs offshore wind now under £40/MWh
    • Nuclear takes 10-15 years to build vs 2-3 years for equivalent renewable capacity
    • No solution for permanent nuclear waste storage after 70 years of nuclear power
    • Renewables with storage now cheaper and faster to deploy than nuclear
    Sources: National Audit Office, Department for Business Energy & Industrial Strategy, Committee on Radioactive Waste Management
  • "Electric vehicles aren't practical" - Evidence shows rapid improvement and adoption
    • EV range now 200-400 miles, covering 99% of UK journeys (average 20 miles daily)
    • UK has 42,000 charge points, growing at 35% annually, with rapid chargers at 80% motorway services
    • Total cost of ownership now lower for EVs than petrol/diesel vehicles
    • Norway shows rapid transition possible - 80% new car sales electric with comprehensive policy support
    • Green policy focuses on reducing car dependency through better public transport and active travel
    Sources: Department for Transport, Zap-Map, RAC Foundation, Norwegian Electric Vehicle Association
  • "Public ownership doesn't work" - Evidence shows it often works better
    • Publicly owned Scottish Water charges 20% less than English private companies with better service
    • Publicly owned East Coast Mainline returned £1 billion to treasury vs private failures
    • 83% of UK water companies owned by private equity, extracting £2.4 billion annually in dividends
    • Public ownership in energy enables coordinated transition to renewables and lower consumer bills
    • 70% of UK public support public ownership of key utilities (We Own It polling)
    Sources: We Own It, University of Greenwich Public Services International Research Unit, National Audit Office, YouGov
  • "Four-day week isn't practical" - Trials show benefits for productivity and wellbeing
    • UK trials show 20% productivity increase with four-day week on same pay
    • Reduces unemployment by sharing work more evenly across workforce
    • Improves work-life balance, mental health, and gender equality in domestic labor
    • Iceland trial with 2,500 workers showed maintained or improved productivity in majority workplaces
    • Technological advancement means we can produce more with less human labor
    Sources: 4 Day Week Campaign, Autonomy research group, Association for Sustainable Democracy (Iceland), University of Cambridge
  • "Universal Basic Income is unaffordable" - Multiple funding models show feasibility
    • Pilot programs worldwide show improved health, education, and entrepreneurship outcomes
    • Can be funded through progressive taxation, carbon taxes, and financial transaction taxes
    • Replaces complex benefits system reducing administrative costs
    • Provides security during automation transition and enables retraining
    • Finnish trial showed improved wellbeing and stress reduction despite employment effects
    Sources: Basic Income Earth Network, Stanford Basic Income Lab, Finnish Government UBI Evaluation, Royal Society of Arts
  • "Green policies have never worked anywhere" - Multiple successful international examples exist
    • German Energiewende created 300,000 renewable energy jobs while reducing emissions
    • Norwegian EV policy resulted in 80% new car sales being electric through comprehensive incentives
    • Costa Rica generates 99% renewable electricity for past 8 years
    • Portuguese four-day week trial showed 25% productivity increase with reduced absenteeism
    • Multiple US cities with $15 minimum wage show no significant job losses as opponents predicted
    Sources: German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs, Costa Rican Institute of Electricity, University of California Berkeley Labor Center
  • "UK is too small to make a difference" - UK influence and historical responsibility matter
    • UK is 5th largest historical emitter - moral responsibility to lead on solutions
    • UK innovation and finance sector gives disproportionate global influence
    • Demonstrating successful green transition inspires other nations to follow
    • Climate change requires all countries to act - no "free rider" option exists
    • UK soft power and diplomatic influence can multiply impact of domestic policies
    Sources: Carbon Brief, World Resources Institute, Foreign Policy Centre, Chatham House
  • "Previous green policies failed" - Most were inadequately implemented or underfunded
    • UK Green Deal failed due to complex administration and high interest rates, not the concept
    • German solar success shows comprehensive policy with proper funding works
    • UK offshore wind success resulted from consistent government support and auctions
    • Green policies work when implemented comprehensively with adequate funding and cross-party support
    • Piecemeal approaches without whole-system thinking inevitably underperform
    Sources: National Audit Office, Energy & Climate Intelligence Unit, UK Green Building Council, Agora Energiewende
